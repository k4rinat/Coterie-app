Why no accent colour

Every site in the luxury fashion space uses a monochrome palette. Colour is a signal of mass-market. For Coterie, the restraint is the brand.

Warm neutrals not cold

Each grey has a very slight warm undertone. Compared to pure #f4f4f4, the warmth reads as considered — not just default grey.

Token system

Every colour is a CSS variable. Nothing is hardcoded anywhere. Changing the palette means changing one file — every component updates automatically.

Why Cormorant

The Row and Lemaire both use a similar weight of editorial serif. Cormorant at weight 300 reads as designed — not defaulted. It also has genuine optical sizing support, which makes large display text feel crisp.

Why Inter

Inter at weight 300 sits quiet next to Cormorant. It never competes. Weight 500 is only used for small-caps labels where readability matters more than elegance. Nothing heavier, anywhere.

Spacing

Spacing is what makes the restraint visible.

1 4px Between inline elements, icon and label
2 8px Label to content beneath it
4 16px Gap between list items and table rows
6 24px Card padding, inline group gap
12 48px Between content blocks within a section
24 96px Section vertical padding — py-24 on every section
py-24 (96px) on every section is the single most important spacing decision. It creates the consistent rhythm that makes the page feel considered rather than packed.

Visual Rules

What we decided to always do. And never do.

No box shadows

Box shadows make things look like they're floating. Everything on Coterie sits flat on the page. Elevation is communicated through background colour difference, not shadows.

Used
Not used

No filled buttons

Every call to action is a hairline text link. The underline is the only indicator. Filled buttons read as product-app, not fashion-platform. The restraint is part of the brand.

Dividers not cards

Content is separated by horizontal rules, not card containers. Cards add visual weight and imply clickability. For content that isn't interactive, rules are cleaner and quieter.
