Stage 01 / Research & Vision Who is this for. What does it need to feel like.

Coterie is not a marketplace.
It is a membership.

The starting point wasn't a list of features. It was a question: what does a platform look like when the people on it are already established? No portfolio-building. No public profiles. No cold outreach. The website had to communicate exclusivity before anyone read a single word.

The brief

Build a website for a private fashion industry network. Two audiences — creative freelancers, and the luxury brands that hire them. Access by application only.

The tone

Not a startup. Not a job board. Closer to a members' club. The website should feel like receiving an invitation, not downloading an app.

The constraint

Nothing on the site should feel loud. No bold claims. No hero images. No bright buttons. The restraint is the signal.

Users

Two very different people.
One website.

Persona A
The Creative

Creative Freelancer — Stylist, Photographer, Art Director

Experience 8–15 years. Already established. Not looking to build a portfolio.
Problem No platform speaks to their level. Everything feels junior or too public.
Fear Being seen on a platform that cheapens what they do.
Need from site To feel like this is built for them. Private. Serious. Not mass-market.
Persona B
The Brand

Fashion House, Luxury Agency, PR Firm — Creative Director or Head of Production

Context Hires creative talent frequently. Needs discretion. No public casting.
Problem Agencies are slow and expensive. Word-of-mouth only goes so far.
Fear Wrong person attached to the brand. Risk to reputation.
Need from site Confidence that every person here has been vetted. No noise.

Goals

What the website must achieve.

01

Communicate exclusivity immediately

Before the user reads anything, the design itself — the whitespace, the typeface, the restraint — has to signal that this is not for everyone.

02

Explain the model clearly

Two different audiences need to understand how it works. Not through long copy. Through structure — a logical flow from landing to application.

03

Drive a single action

The only goal is the application. Every section, every heading, every CTA points to one place. Nothing competes with it.

References

What we looked at before designing anything.

The Row

Primary reference — full aesthetic

The most important reference. Total restraint. No images on the homepage. Typography as the only design element. Every spacing decision communicates quality without saying a word.

Lemaire

Tone and spacing

Generous line height. Body copy treated as a luxury signal — not a footnote. The navigation is so understated you almost miss it. Taught us how to use silence.

Toteme

Grid and layout rhythm

Strict column structure. Horizontal rules used as the only visual dividers. Consistent section-to-section rhythm. No section tries to be more important than any other.

Jil Sander

Copywriting register

Short, declarative sentences. Nothing explained twice. Trust implied, never stated. The copy doesn't convince — it informs. That's the register we aimed for.

Design directions adopted

✓Maximum white space — the layout breathes
✓Serif at weight 300 — quiet, not authoritative
✓No hero images — the headline is the visual
✓Horizontal rules as the only dividers
✓No accent colour — everything is black, white, grey
✓Short copy — nothing over two sentences per section

Design directions rejected

✗Photography-led hero — wrong for a service platform
✗Filled CTA buttons — too loud, too product-app
✗Gradient backgrounds — decorative, adds no meaning
✗Icon sets — visual noise, doesn't fit the register
✗Social proof carousels — undermines the exclusivity
✗Showing pricing upfront — premature, wrong order
