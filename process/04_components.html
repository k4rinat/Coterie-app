Stage 04 / Component Design Every UI element planned before it was built.

UI Components

Each component designed with one purpose.
Nothing more than it needs.

Navigation Bar

The nav is always visible. It never competes with the page. Wordmark left, links floating in the middle, Apply CTA right. No dropdown, no mega-menu.

Decided: No hamburger on desktop. No dropdowns. No mega-menu. The site is small enough that everything fits flat.

Hero Section

Full viewport height. One headline, one CTA. No supporting image, no subtext, no bullets. The first thing you read is the most important thing on the page.

Preview

Where Fashion
Trusts Talent.

Apply for access →

Decided: The original version had three supporting bullet points and a background grid. Both were removed — the headline alone was stronger.

Pillars

Three value propositions in a horizontal row. No heading above them, no background change. They appear immediately after the hero with no visual separation — continuous reading.

Preview

Verified

Every member has been reviewed and approved by the Coterie team.

Private

No public profiles. No searchable directory. Introductions only.

Curated

We accept fewer than 20% of applications. Quality by design.

Decided: No icons next to each pillar. The words don't need illustration — adding icons would make it look like a feature comparison chart.

Disciplines List

All creative disciplines on the platform listed in two columns with ruled rows. This section does one thing: make visitors see their own job title and feel recognised.

Decided: Listed alphabetically within category, not by prominence. No creative should feel ranked lower than another.

Membership Tier Cards

Three tiers for creative members — Emerging, Professional, Elite. All presented equally in a row. No highlighted "recommended" tier. No filled background on any card. Border only.

Decided: Three tiers — Emerging (£20, with a 7-day free trial), Professional (£50), Elite (£100). Professional is marked "Most Popular" but no card is filled or highlighted above others.

Brand Access Tier Cards

Three annual plans for brands — Starter, Growth, Enterprise. Priced yearly rather than monthly to signal a longer-term partnership. Same border-only treatment as creative cards. No tier highlighted above the others.

Decided: Annual pricing (not monthly) signals commitment and aligns with how brands budget for talent. Enterprise uses "Contact us" rather than a fixed price — the value scales with the organisation.

Footer

Full-width black. Wordmark on the left, nav links in the centre, copyright right. Minimal. No second nav, no social links, no newsletter form.
