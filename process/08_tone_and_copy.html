Stage 08 / Tone & Copy The language decisions — how the site speaks, and why.

Voice Principles

The site speaks the way
the brand wants to be perceived.

01

Confident, not boastful

Coterie doesn't shout about being exclusive. It simply is. The copy states things plainly without superlatives or hype. "A private network for fashion creatives" — not "the #1 platform for fashion professionals."

02

No exclamation marks. Ever.

Exclamation marks communicate excitement, which communicates insecurity. Every sentence ends with a full stop or nothing. This was a hard rule set from day one and never broken across any page.

03

Short sentences. Space to breathe.

No paragraph longer than three sentences on any hero or intro section. Long copy signals a need to persuade. The site never persuades — it informs. The user decides.

04

Gatekeeping without arrogance

"Not everyone is accepted" appears on the site — but only once, and without ceremony. The tone is matter-of-fact, not self-congratulatory. The bar exists for quality, not prestige.

05

Active, not passive

Every CTA is a verb. "Apply." "Start free trial." "Discover the disciplines." The user always knows exactly what will happen next. No vague "Learn more" or "Find out more" buttons anywhere.

06

Value before price

The homepage never mentions cost. The how it works page never mentions cost. Cost appears only on the pricing page — after the user already understands what they're getting. Price is never introduced before value.

Language Do / Don't

Specific words were either used
or deliberately avoided.

Used — and why it works
Avoided — and why it was rejected
"Apply" — implies selection, not sign-up. The user earns access.
"Join" / "Sign up" / "Register" — frictionless language that implies anyone can get in.
"Vetted" — factual. It describes a process, not a value judgement.
"Elite" / "Exclusive" / "Premium" — self-applied prestige language feels hollow.
"Private network" — accurate. The network is private by design.
"Community" / "Tribe" / "Family" — over-familiar, soft, used by every startup.
"Fashion creatives" — broad, inclusive of all disciplines and levels.
"Fashion professionals" / "Industry insiders" — either too corporate or too self-important.
"Introductions" — specific. It describes exactly what happens between members.
"Connections" / "Networking" — overused in every professional platform context.
"How it works" — clear, functional, no pretence.
"Our story" / "Our mission" / "About us" — generic, inward-facing.
"For brands" — direct, unambiguous. Brands know immediately where to go.
"Partners" / "Collaborators" / "Enterprise" — softens the transactional reality.
